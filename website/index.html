    The interactive plots showcase the production of coffee across different continents and countries over a span of several decades. While coffee is grown in various parts of the world, these visualizations reveal that certain regions have emerged as leaders in coffee production.

    One striking finding from the choropleth map is the dominance of Brazil in global coffee production. The map highlights how Brazil's coffee production has steadily grown over the years, and how it now accounts for a significant proportion of the world's coffee supply. Moreover, the map also shows the emergence of other countries such as Vietnam, which has risen to become the second-largest coffee producer in the world. By providing a visual representation of coffee production at a global level, these visualizations underscore the significance of these countries in meeting the world's demand for coffee.

    The line plots provide additional insights into coffee production at a more granular level. By allowing viewers to explore production trends for specific countries and continents over time, these plots highlight the complexities of coffee production and how it varies across different regions. For instance, the line plot for Asia reveals that coffee production in this region has remained relatively flat over the years, while the line plot for Africa shows a more erratic trend with periods of rapid growth followed by declines in production.     A Flavor Expedition: Delving into the Coffee Tasting Profiles of Leading Countries

    As we continue our journey, let's embark on a flavor expedition to understand the complexity of coffee flavor profiles. By analyzing data from a global coffee tasting competition, we can identify the countries that produce the highest-rated coffees and the distinct flavor attributes that set them apart. Our interactive bubble plot showcases the top coffee-producing countries, with the size of the bubbles indicating the average rating of their coffees. Notably, Ethiopia, Uganda, and Kenya emerge as the highest-rated coffee producers.

    To delve deeper into what makes these coffees so exceptional, we've used a radar plot to visualize their flavor attributes, such as acidity, sweetness, and body. For example, Ethiopian coffees stand out in every aspect, although most countries' coffee beans perform well in terms of uniformity, clean up and sweetness, Ugandan coffee beans are particularly exceptional when it comes to Aroma. If you enjoy coffee with a rich and captivating aroma, now you know which country's beans to seek out. For first-time coffee drinkers, we recommend trying Colombian coffee beans, as their balanced profile makes them an excellent starting point. By examining these visualizations, we can better appreciate the complexity of coffee flavors and the significant role geography plays in shaping them.
