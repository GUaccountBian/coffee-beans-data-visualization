    Exploring the Fascinating World of Coffee Bean Production

    The interactive plots above showcase the production of coffee across different continents and countries over a span of several decades. While coffee is grown in various parts of the world, these visualizations reveal that certain regions have emerged as leaders in coffee production. One striking finding from the choropleth map is the dominance of Brazil in global coffee production. The map highlights how Brazil's coffee production has steadily grown over the years, and how it now accounts for a significant proportion of the world's coffee supply. Moreover, the map also shows the emergence of other countries such as Vietnam, which has risen to become the second-largest coffee producer in the world. By providing a visual representation of coffee production at a global level, these visualizations underscore the significance of these countries in meeting the world's demand for coffee. The line plots provide additional insights into coffee production at a more granular level. By allowing viewers to explore production trends for specific countries and continents over time, these plots highlight the complexities of coffee production and how it varies across different regions. For instance, the line plot for Asia reveals that coffee production in this region has remained relatively flat over the years, while the line plot for Africa shows a more erratic trend with periods of rapid growth followed by declines in production.     Coffee Tasting Profiles of Selected Countries

    To understand the complexity of coffee flavor profiles, we turned to data from a global coffee tasting competition. By analyzing this data, we were able to identify the countries that produce the highest-rated coffees, as well as the flavor attributes that make them stand out. Our interactive bubble plot shows the top coffee-producing countries, with the size of the bubbles representing average rating of their coffees. We can see that Ethiopia, Uganda, and Kenya are the highest-rated coffee producers.

    To further understand what makes these coffees stand out, we used a radar plot to visualize their flavor attributes, such as acidity, sweetness, and body. We can see that Ethiopian coffees have high acidity, while Colombian coffees have a balanced flavor profile with moderate levels of all attributes. Now, if you like acidic, you know what country of coffee bean you need to buy. If you are a first time coffee drinker, I suggest you drink coffee made from Colombian coffee beans.
