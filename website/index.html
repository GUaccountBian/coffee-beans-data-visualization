    A Flavor Expedition: Delving into the Coffee Tasting Profiles of Leading Countries

    As you take a closer look at the bag of coffee beans, you wonder what it will taste like. Will it be too acidic or too dark? Once again, the interactive bubble plot comes to your rescue. The size of each bubble represents the average rating of each coffee bean, and when you click on a bubble, you are presented with a radar plot showing its flavor attributes. Now you know exactly what you're in for.

    To understand the complexity of coffee flavor profiles, we turned to data from a global coffee tasting competition. By analyzing this data, we were able to identify the countries that produce the highest-rated coffees, as well as the flavor attributes that make them stand out. Our interactive bubble plot shows the top coffee-producing countries, with the size of the bubbles representing average rating of their coffees. We can see that Ethiopia, Uganda, and Kenya are the highest-rated coffee producers.

    To further understand what makes these coffees stand out, we used a radar plot to visualize their flavor attributes, such as acidity, sweetness, and body. We can see that Ethiopian coffees have high acidity, while Colombian coffees have a balanced flavor profile with moderate levels of all attributes. Now, if you like acidic, you know what country of coffee bean you need to buy. If you are a first time coffee drinker, I suggest you drink coffee made from Colombian coffee beans. You can't go wrong that way. By combining these visualizations,we can gain a deeper appreciation for the complexity of coffee flavor and the important role that geography plays in shaping it.

    Cultivating Connections: The Intricate Links between Coffee Varieties and Their Countries of Origin

    Now, if you are deciding that you want the coffee bean from Brazil? No problem. The chord diagram on the right displays all the different varieties of coffee beans, and on the left, you can see the countries associated with each variety. The darker the color, the more prevalent the association. With this wealth of information at your fingertips, you feel empowered and confident in your ability to choose the perfect coffee beans to suit your taste.

    This visualization built upon a foundation of coffee bean reviews, provides a visual representation of the relationship between countries and the varieties of coffee beans they produce. By using this diagram, coffee lovers can easily identify which countries produce the types of coffee beans they enjoy and discover new varieties they may not have tried before.By hovering over a specific arc unveils the corresponding connections, shedding light on the magnitude of each country's contribution to the global coffee production.

    As you can see, Typica, Caturra and Bourbon have associated with many countries. FYI, they are all from the species of Arabica.
