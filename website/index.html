    The interactive plots above showcase the production of coffee across different continents and countries over a span of several decades. While coffee is grown in various parts of the world, these visualizations reveal that certain regions have emerged as leaders in coffee production. One striking finding from the choropleth map is the dominance of Brazil in global coffee production. The map highlights how Brazil's coffee production has steadily grown over the years, and how it now accounts for a significant proportion of the world's coffee supply. Moreover, the map also shows the emergence of other countries such as Vietnam, which has risen to become the second-largest coffee producer in the world. By providing a visual representation of coffee production at a global level, these visualizations underscore the significance of these countries in meeting the world's demand for coffee. The line plots provide additional insights into coffee production at a more granular level. By allowing viewers to explore production trends for specific countries and continents over time, these plots highlight the complexities of coffee production and how it varies across different regions. For instance, the line plot for Africa reveals that coffee production in this region has remained relatively flat over the years, while the line plot for Asia shows a more erratic trend with periods of rapid growth followed by declines in production. Overall, the interactive plots demonstrate the importance of coffee as a global commodity and highlight the key players in the coffee production industry. By enabling viewers to explore coffee production data in an engaging and interactive way, these visualizations provide valuable insights into the forces that shape coffee production and consumption around the world.

    Coffee Tasting Profiles of Selected Countries

    Coffee is one of the most popular drinks in the world, with millions of people enjoying it daily. However, the flavor of coffee can vary widely depending on where it's grown and how it's roasted. To understand the complexity of coffee flavor profiles, we turned to data from a global coffee tasting competition. By analyzing this data, we were able to identify the countries that produce the highest-rated coffees, as well as the flavor attributes that make them stand out. Our interactive bubble plot shows the top coffee-producing countries, with the size of the bubbles representing average rating of their coffees. We can see that Ethiopia, Uganda, and Kenya are the highest-rated coffee producers.

    To further understand what makes these coffees stand out, we used a radar plot to visualize their flavor attributes, such as acidity, sweetness, and body. We can see that Ethiopian coffees have high acidity, while Colombian coffees have a balanced flavor profile with moderate levels of all attributes. By combining these visualizations, we can gain a deeper appreciation for the complexity of coffee flavor and the important role that geography plays in shaping it.

    This visualization built upon a foundation of coffee bean reviews, employes the artistry of arcs and ribbons to illuminate the intricate web of connections between countries and their cherished coffee varieties.
